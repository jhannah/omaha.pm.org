Cool stuff! Here's $0.02 from the peanut gallery of my twisted noggin',
if you want it:
- What if you want a delimiter other than whitespace (the default
delimiter of Perl's split function)? You could add a -d option to
specify the delimiter, like "cut" allows you to do from the command
line. Further, you could allow the user to pass a regex into -d if they
want! Try that mojo w/ "cut"! -grin-
- The program is only 100 lines long, and the use of globals is already
making me nervous. If the program gets much bigger, your global use of
@nfl and such is probably going to get really hard to maintain quickly.
I'd "use strict;" and return @nfl from your call to parse_field_list(),
etc.
- Wow. "$#f". Ya know, I've been coding Perl for almost 10 years and
I've never used $# in an actual program. I don't know how I've gotten
away with that. -grin- I didn't even know what $# did until I was on a
chat room a few months ago. We can use this for a quiz for the other
Perl Monger lurkers out there to see if *they* know what it does. -evil
laugh-
- Why is it called "f"? Why not "f.pl"? Gotta give props to the Perl
deities, ya know. Good karma.

And I'm not even drunk. -grin-
