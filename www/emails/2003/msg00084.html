I was just checking through the pm.org site and saw the omaha.pm has
been 'resurected'.  :)   My name is Jeff Bisbee and I'm the
SouthFlorida.pm group leader (http://southflorida.pm.org).  I'm also 
working on this year's YAPC conference @ Florida Atlantic University in 
Boca Raton, FL.

I grew up in Omaha and went to the University of Nebraksa at Omaha and
figured I could help the group out.  I was googling the other day and
found this post:

    http://omaha.pm.org/emails/1998-2002/msg00007.html

I also would like to thank Matt Payne for "showing me the path" so long
ago.  Back in 1995 I was attempting my first web stuff and ran all over
UNO's campus with everyone telling me to take a hike (because I was
bugging the hell out of everyone by asking vauge questions).  This
search led me to Matt's office where he gave me a temporary account on
his personal Linux box under his desk gave me some great advice.  He
basically told me to read as much as I could, grab everything I could
get my hands on, then I ran into a specific question, ask it, get the
answer, then keep on reading.  I passed that lesson on many times and as
I reflect, it was some of the best advice I've ever gotten.

Also I have an attempt at a blog/photos/etc at jbisbee.com, but I've
been pretty lazy about it lately, and haven't updated in the while.

I'm also a member of Florida Linux Users eXchange (flux.org) a south
florida LUG and have given a talk on installing Debian.  If you want to
check it you, you can go here:

    http://www.flux.org/mtgnotes/feb2002/index.html

BTW, you might find mailman to be better than majordomo (yes I know that
its written in python) but it blocks spam and is web driven.  Send a
message to Wayne Walker if you want to switch (I have his e-mail
someplace - so bug me if you need it)  Wayne is the pm.org admin type
guy.

Well I've rambled enough and need to get back to work :)  Are any of you
guys attending YAPC?
