>>What is it?
The Campaign for Real Ale is/was a movement afoot in the UK to get people
drinking stuff beyond Bugwhiter or Bugwhiter Lite. It's gotten overrun with
Labourists and become more concerned with administration than effectiveness
from what my associates in the Isles tell me.

>>What is real beer?
Beer is technically any fermented beverage made mostly from water and malted
cereal grains (unless you're in Texas, where Beer is Ale and Lager is
Lager.) And yes, although Sake is commonly misknown as Rice Wine, it's
really beer.

In most knowledge folks opinion, however, that "real" beer has only
ingredients:
* Malted barley or Wheat or both
* Hops
* Water
* Yeast (although this wasn't an understood part of the fermentation
processing until much later)

In fact, the Germans were so fanatical about this purity that they codified
it as a law (the Reinheitsgebot.) Most of the American macrobrews products
contain "adjuncts" such as rice and corn, thus making "less than real beer."


Despite all this blather, I'm left wondering how, as a Nihilist, I really
should answer that question. <grin>

>>What is an Ale?
A sub-class of beer fermented by yeasts of the Saccharomyces Cerevisiae
species. So called "Top-Fermenting" yeast, fermentation occurs between
between 60 and 75 degrees. The other significant sub-class is lager, which
is fermented by our friends the Saccharomyces Uvarum ("Bottom-Fermenting")
yeast between 35 and 45 degrees.

But then, I could be wrong. After all, there's more than way to brew it. :)
