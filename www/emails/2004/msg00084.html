Well, Tandem is techically no-more -- it's called HP Non-Stop NSK.  But most users still call it "Tandem".  HP still makes them, but no one is buying them.  The rumor is that they are going to phase them out, but that's just a rumor.

Back to the question . . . I've been able to telnet into Tandems many times.  Never tried it with any Perl script, but I can't see why it wouldn't work.

However, I'd describe Tandem's TCPIP as "janky at best".  Once, at a customer's site, the TCP process on the Tandem kept dying and the only way bring it back was to shut the system down and restart it.  They called Tandem support and they advised putting a hub between the Tandem and the rest of the network.  Now, this machine was already on a hub by itself, but that was on the only thing that support would suggest.  So they bought one, and *presto* it worked.  No idea why, only perhaps the TCP process was getting confused by broadcast messages or something . . . 

I think that there is a Perl port for Tandem. I know there is a vi clone (I've used it myself . . .)

good luck!
