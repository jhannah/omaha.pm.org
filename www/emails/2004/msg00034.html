Hello!

  I am thinking about writing a quick and dirty Perl program to split up
some large MP3 files into 5 minute chunks (some of the originals are 45+
minutes long).  There are quite a few hits on Google for MP3 tag and
attribute modules, but nothing that can edit the audio directly (i.e.
fade in/out, equalize the volume, etc).

  Has anyone ever tried doing this in Perl?

Dan

P.S. For background, this is a follow-on to my post to the OLUG list
regarding news audio sites that I can download to my MP3 player.  Since my
Creative MuVo doesn't do fast-forward/rewind it just jumps to the
beginning of the next MP3 file, if I get interrupted 1/2 way through an
hour long show and can't pause, I have to start at the beginning the next
time.  If I could break them up into 5minute chunks (with some fade in and
fade out) I could make it clean enough to listen to, but still be able to
get close to where I stopped listening to it the last time.
