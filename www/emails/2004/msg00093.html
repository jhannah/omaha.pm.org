Weird. Never seen this before. Trying to use CPAN like I always do earlier today

perl -MCPAN -e shell

I kept getting an error about mirrorscentral.com being offline, unable to fetch X. X being whatever I was trying to download or its metadata... So after 'perldoc CPAN' and checking out mirrors.cpan.org I issued these commands:

   o conf urllist pop
   o conf urllist push ftp://ftp.cs.colorado.edu/mirrors/CPAN/

To change my mirrors... Then I

install Bundle::CPAN

thinking that might clear the problem, but it didn't... Nothing on groups.google.com about "core CPAN server" problems or anything, and I didn't see any configurable files in /root/.cpan/*...

Oh well. After changing my urllist (above) everything was fine. Everything except for some reason CPAN was telling me there was a newer version of CPAN available: 1.70. Which is fine, except I found a version 1.76 on CPAN itself...

authors/id/A/AN/ANDK/CPAN-1.76.tar.gz

Very strange. I don't think CPAN had been used on that box for over a year. The mirror dated must get aggressively old after a bit...
