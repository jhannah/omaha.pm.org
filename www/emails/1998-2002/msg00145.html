I just joined the mailing list and noticed that the Omaha Perl Monger
web site states that the next meeting is August 16, 1999.  Since I have
not yet acquired a time machine, I was wondering if the group is still
active and, if so, when and where the next meeting will be.  I would be
happy to lend a hand in updating/maintaining the web site or organizing
the next meeting.

If there is an active Linux User's Group around Omaha, I would also
appreciate any contact information someone is willing to provide.  When
I looked in the official places, I found messages indicating that there
were once people wanting to start Linux User's Groups here but no
evidence that anyone succeeded.

About me:

I moved to Omaha from Seattle about 9 months ago because my wife has a
postdoc at Boystown National Research Hospital.  I completed my Ph.D. in
Applied Mathematics in March at the University of Washington, focussing
on biological oceanography and climate, and now spend my time searching
for what's next.  Perl has been my language of choice for many projects
since 1995, including single-language projects, projects that involve
interaction with other languages and tools, and client-server projects
that are distributed over a network.  (I also have every issue of the
Perl Journal.)  I am mostly a UNIX person and used to be a systems
administrator on large-scale UNIX systems for Boeing.  I am very
interested in open-source software, have used Linux as my primary
operating system since 1994, and have used open-source tools to perform
the computational portions of most of my research.  For more information
about me (but not necessarily my experience with perl), see the URL
below.

Hope there's still somebody out there,

Mark
