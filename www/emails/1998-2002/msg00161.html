And now, for the questions:

1)  I was looking at the code for CGI.pm (*shudder*) and saw this syntax:

$AUTOLOADED_ROUTINES=<<'END_OF_AUTOLOAD';

%SUBS = (
    'sub1' => <<'END_OF_FUNCTION',
sub my_sub { # do something }
END_OF_FUNCTION
)
END_OF_AUTOLOAD

which, after a lot of magic happened, was used in conjunction with AUTOLOAD,
and called like:

my $auto = \${"$pack\:\:AUTOLOADED_ROUTINES"};
eval "package $pack; $$auto";
$$auto = '';

My first response was WTFO?!  (Which, translated, means 'What the...')

After looking at it a few times, drinking some Diet Mountain Dew, scratching
my head, and staring some more, I figured out what it was doing, and -- to a
degree -- how it was happening.

I've used AUTOLOADER in the past to create on-the-fly accessor methods for
objects, and we also discussed them at our last meeting.

However, this is the first time I've seen it used to create subroutines!

Now, could someone please explain the other answers:  Why use that somewhat
obfuscated and not easily comprehensible method?  Where and when would you use
it, as opposed to a normal subroutine?
