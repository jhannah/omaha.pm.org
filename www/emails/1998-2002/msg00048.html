ktb <xyf@xxxxxxxxxxxx> writes:

> I've been led to believe that it's possible to use gmtime to find
> the number of seconds (since Jan. 1 1970) for an arbitrary date.  In
> other words I would like to use gmtime to find the number of seconds
> that will have ticked by from 1/1/70 to 1/1/2000.  I looked at the
> man page "perlfunc" which has a section on "gmtime" but I can't see
> where it lists what I would like to do.  Anyone know how to do this?

The Perl module Date::Parse could probably take the string "January 1,
2000" and convert it to the number of seconds since the epoch
(remember time zone offsets).

A brute force way would be to take 29 * 365 * 24 * 60 * 60 seconds
from the epoch and just keep counting up until gmtime()'s year rolled
over to 2000:

  $secs = 29 * 365 * 24 * 60 * 60;  # Starting date/time
  $incr = 24 * 60 * 60;  # increment by days

  ($sec,$min,$hour,$mday,$mon,$year,$wday,$yday,$isdst) = gmtime($secs);
  # gmtime() returns the number of years since 1900
  while ($year != 100) {
    $secs += $incr;
    ($sec,$min,$hour,$mday,$mon,$year,$wday,$yday,$isdst) = gmtime($secs);
  }
  print "Seconds: $secs\n";
  print "Time:    " . gmtime($secs) . "\n";
