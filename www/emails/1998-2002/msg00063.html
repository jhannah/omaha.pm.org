Aaron Grothe wrote:

> Hi,
> 
> Has anyone experimented with this before?
> The URL is
> 
> http://www.demobuilder.com/perl2exe.htm
> 
> It's supposed to take a perl program and generate an executable under Windows/NT or Unix that doesn't require any support files, in most cases.

Aaron,

I have no direct experience, but from what I've heard on various mailing
lists and newsgroups, what you end up with is a pretty large executable.
Supposedly improvements in performance are pretty much a wash as it
takes longer to load the big binary.

And this executable will only do the one thing that the original script
was designed to do ... if you wanted to have multiple compiled scripts,
they each would have their own compiled Perl environment ... at least
that's the way I understand it. This may be what you want, if you know
this will be a one shot deal or if you want to hide your source code.

It's never really appealed to me ... I feel like the money to be made is
in providing a service ... an ability to make desirable things happen.
An ability to have your stuff work with other stuff that you may not
have anticipated ... your employer may disagree! 
Come to think of it, *my* employer may disagree!

Give it a try and tell us about it!
